The Club

In January 2022, I co-founded a CNC club in Marblehead, MA with the goal of learning how to use the wood shop's new CNC machine. This meant the club had to start from the ground up.

Over six months the club completed two full projects — an electric guitar body milled from wood and an aluminum Octo-grid — each requiring CAD modeling, CAM toolpath generation, machine setup, and post-processing of the finished parts.

This was my first real introduction to subtractive manufacturing and the precision required to translate a digital design into a machined part. The experience directly shaped how I approach fabrication projects today.

The Projects

Project 01 · Wood

Electric Guitar

The guitar was the club's first major project. Starting from reference dimensions of a standard electric guitar body, a CAD model was built and the body shape, pickup cavities, and neck pocket were all modeled before any cutting began.

Toolpaths were generated in CAM software, accounting for bit diameter, stepover, depth of cut, and feeds and speeds appropriate for wood. The CNC machine then milled the entire body — contours, cavities, and all — from a wood blank.

The result was a fully shaped electric guitar body, ready for hardware installation and finishing.

Project 02 · Aluminum

Octo-Grid

The second project stepped up the difficulty by switching from wood to 7075-T651 aluminum — a much harder material that demands tighter tolerances, lower feeds, and careful fixturing to avoid chatter or tool breakage.

The Octo-grid design features a repeating geometric pattern milled into the aluminum plate. Each pocket required precise depth control and consistent toolpaths to achieve a clean, uniform finish across the part.

Machining aluminum for the first time was a significant learning experience — understanding coolant, chip evacuation, and the difference in feel between cutting wood and metal.

Aluminum Milling 7075-T651 Fixturing Geometric Pattern

What I Learned

  • How to take a CAD model all the way through to a finished machined part
  • G-code fundamentals and CAM toolpath strategy
  • The difference between machining wood and aluminum — feeds, speeds, fixturing
  • Machine setup, zeroing, and work coordinate systems
  • How to co-found and organize a technical club from scratch

What I'd Do Differently

  • More finish passes on the aluminum to improve surface quality
  • Better fixturing strategy to reduce vibration on the guitar body
  • Document toolpaths and setups more carefully for repeatability
